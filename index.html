// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your project portfolio images—spanning multi-family housing, historic renovations, and hospitality work in Portland—are almost certainly deployed as decorative files with no JSON-LD schema, no structured `ImageObject` markup, and no descriptive alt-text tying them to location or typology. Google cannot read "Pearl District mixed-use renovation" or "Portland custom home architect" from a blank alt attribute. You are bleeding the query "historic renovation architect Portland Oregon" to competitors whose images are tagged, geolocated, and schema-wrapped as indexed assets.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: A commercial real estate developer or hospitality group evaluating you for a multi-family or hotel project will leave without contacting you because your portfolio presents project types as a list—"multi-family housing, offices, hospitality"—with zero visual proof differentiating your execution quality from any other mid-tier firm. Your stated identity as a studio that produces work "worthy of its place" is a written claim completely unsupported by process documentation, material detail shots, or before/after visual sequencing. That buyer needs proof before they pick up the phone, and you are offering them a philosophy statement instead.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: A sharp Portland competitor targeting the same custom home and hospitality segments is winning project inquiries by publishing construction-phase visual documentation—framing progress, material selection boards, site context photography—that demonstrates process credibility, not just finished results. Your current portfolio presents endpoints with no narrative architecture around how you got there, which means a prospective hospitality or custom home client comparing firms assigns lower perceived rigor to your studio. That gap is directly costing you first-contact conversions on projects where budget and trust threshold are both high.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured visual capture protocol across three to four representative project types—at minimum one historic renovation and one multi-family—that produces site-context exteriors, material-detail close-ups, interior spatial sequences, and one construction-phase documentation set per project. Every image file delivered must carry embedded EXIF location data, standardized filename conventions (`portland-pearl-district-multifamily-exterior-01.jpg`), descriptive alt-text mapped to target queries, and JSON-LD `ImageObject` schema deployed on every portfolio page at launch. This turns your existing portfolio investment into a structured, crawlable asset library that ranks, builds process credibility, and closes the trust gap your written copy cannot close alone.
